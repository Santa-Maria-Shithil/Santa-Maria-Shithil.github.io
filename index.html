I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science at Stony Brook University (SBU) , New York. Currently, I work with Prof. Shuai Mu, Prof. Anshul Gandhi, and Prof. Erez Zadok. We are working on desiging a hybrid consistency protocol that unify linearizability and causal consistency in a single system.

My research interest lies in designing efficient distributed systems while ensuring correctness through formal verification techniques. I am also interested in computer networking research, particularly problems related to network performance, reliability, and their implications for distributed system design.

Before joining SBU, I completed my M.Sc. (2022) and B.Sc. (2016) in Computer Science and Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) and Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU), respectively.

Apart from reading system research papers :), I really love reading books — both fiction and non-fiction. Although work kept me away from books for a while, I have committed to reading regularly again—even on the busiest days. You can check my GoodReads profile to see what I read, what’s next on my list, and my reviews.
