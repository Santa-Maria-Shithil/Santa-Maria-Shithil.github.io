SM

I am a graduate student in the Department of Computer Science at Stony Brook University (SBU), New York, where I work on the Sustainability Aware Software Systems (SASSY) project under the supervision of Prof. Shuai Mu, Prof. Anshul Gandhi, and Prof. Erez Zadok. As part of this work, I designed a hybrid consistency protocol that unifies linearizability and causal consistency within a single system to reduce latency in geo-distributed systems.

My research interests lie broadly in systems and networking:

  • Distributed Systems — designing efficient, correct distributed systems with formal verification techniques
  • Network Performance — improving reliability and performance, and understanding their implications for distributed system design
  • Systems for ML — building infrastructure that enhances the performance and efficiency of machine learning workloads

Before joining SBU, I completed my B.Sc. (2016) in Computer Science and Engineering at Jatiya Kabi Kazi Nazrul Islam University (JKKNIU), respectively.

Outside of research, I am an avid reader of both fiction and non-fiction. Although work kept me away from books for a while, I have committed to reading regularly again—even on the busiest days. You can check my GoodReads profile to see what I read, what’s next on my list, and my reviews.
